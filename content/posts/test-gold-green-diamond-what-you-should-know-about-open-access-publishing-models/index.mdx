Between 1950 and 1980, the new model of large commercial publishers came to dominate numerous fields of scientific publishing in western countries.The small society presses, struggling to cope with growing scale, were supported and then largely supplanted the 'Big 5' commercial presses: Elsevier (which acquired Pergamon in 1991), Wiley, Springer, Taylor & Francis and Sage. These newly-empowered players brought an industrial approach to the publication and dissemination process. These commercial publishers represent 40% of global scientific publishing market
